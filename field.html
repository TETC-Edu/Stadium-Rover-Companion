Field Reference

The field is 100×100 cm. Three zones live on it — you decide where. Distance is measured from the rover to each zone in millimeters. Turn is measured from the rover's current direction — how far left or right does it need to spin to face the zone? The example below shows ONE possible layout. Yours will look different.

Turning · left or right

How to think about the turn

Stand behind the rover and look where it's pointing. That's forward.

If the zone you want to deliver to is on the rover's left side, you turn LEFT. If it's on the right side, you turn RIGHT. The number is how many degrees.

You're using a protractor on the rover to measure this — not doing math in your head.

The translation · field to code

Once you have the numbers, every delivery turns into two lines:

Python
robot.turn_for(RIGHT, 30)
robot.move_for(450, 0)
Reads as — turn right 30 degrees, then drive straight 450 millimeters.

How to measure each delivery

1
Place the ruler
From the rover's center to the zone marker.
2
Read distance
In cm. Multiply by 10 to get millimeters.
3
Stand behind the rover
Look at where it's pointing — that's forward.
4
Read the turn
Is the zone on the rover's left or right? Use your protractor to measure how many degrees.
Remember: distance is in millimeters (cm × 10). Turn is in degrees, either LEFT or RIGHT.
